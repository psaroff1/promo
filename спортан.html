Sports and Health 

        Since “Okean” was opened, a lot has been done for children’s physical education and children’s sports development. The Center has gained rich experience and has built a great infrastructure. There is a modern swimming-pool with 6 lanes, where children are taught to swim and dive with the help of underwater swimming device “Power Dive”. There is a Tennis Hall with both table tennis tables and a tennis court which can be easily transformed into two badminton courts. Ice Palace has a special coating made of synthetic ice. Sports and fitness complex is intended for fitness and athletics, gymnastics, volleyball, basketball, handball and indoor soccer. There is a large football stadium with a special coating to reduce risks of traumas, four outdoor volleyball and basketball courts, playgrounds and an outdoor gym.

    In 2016 the Technical and Watersports Department was open. It runs the new sports sections, such as:

    – Lazertag,

    – Riding a Segway,

    – Safe ATV driving on a specially fitted track under the guidance of experienced instructors,

    – Workout,

   – Cross fit,

    – Laser shooting saloon where children go through some basic shooting training in the form of a game,

    – Electrical scooters,

    – Orient-show,

    Very soon “Okean” visitors will be able to hone their climbing skills at the adventure park, train at the new stadium of the summer complex “Kityonok- Tigryonok”, and go sailing. At the Sports Palace, children do sambo and judo on a professional wrestling mat that was presented to the Center by Veniamin Shaevich Kaganov, Deputy Minister of Education and Science of the Russian Federation. The Wrestling Festival also takes place here.

    All-Russian Children Center “Okean” is a training ground for many well-known athletic junior teams. Many soccer teams have trained here; for example, «Gangeung Munseong High School Soccer Team» (Republic of Korea), «Yanbian Sports School» (People’s Republic of China), «Albirex» (Niigata, Japan) “Neftyanik” team (Habarovskii Krai, Russia), young hockey players from the junior team “Admiral” and children from the hockey team “Polyus” (Vladivostok, Russia).

    Each year, the participants of the program “Young Rescuers” learn how to act in the emergency situations in the water and practice rescuing people at “Okean”’s swimming pool.
